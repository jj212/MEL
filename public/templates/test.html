1. MERI approach, principles and scope

  1. Approach

The YFBP MERI framework will be operating at two levels:

  1. At the higher level, evaluation activities will be focused on YFBP outcomes and make an overall judgment about the value of the YFBP. This will be provided on an annual basis through the Annual Reports as well as a Summative Evaluation Report in 2021.
  2. At the lower level, monitoring and continuous improvement activities will focus on YFBP outputs and support rapid learning and iteration of the YFBP.

The evaluation team has adopted an utilisation-focused approach to the framework, ensuring that it is useful to the intended users (or audiences). As such, the framework and accompanying KEQs are built around the information needs of the different audiences. The audiences and their information needs are defined in section 2.3 and the KEQs are detailed in Section 3.

We are also informed by a number of principles laid down in the program design and further developed with the YFBP team at the evaluation inception meeting in July 2019. The principles are outlined in 2.2 below.

  1. Principles

There are seven principles that guide the approach and conduct of the evaluation team. These are:

  1. Ownership: it is important that people understand and appreciate why they are doing the things they do and adopt a results-oriented approach to their work.
  2. Continuous improvement: MERI activities are linked to the continuous improvement cycles, ensuring there are feedback loops in place to identify lessons and make appropriate changes.
  3. Stakeholder engagement: the MERI activities will aim to engage stakeholders as broadly as possible to ensure achievements are fully captured and lessons are identified and used to improve performance.
  4. Multiple sources of evidence: MERI reporting incorporates assessment of multiple lines of quantitative and qualitative evidence in order to demonstrate the causal links between activities and outcomes.
  5. Efficiency: investment in MERI activities should be efficient, giving priority to critical information needs and outcomes, making the best use of existing data, and avoiding duplication.
  6. Flexibility: we will remain flexible in our evaluation approach and have built in points during each continuous improvement cycle where the evaluation can iterate as we understand more about the YFBP.
  7. Ethical practices: all MERI activities are consistent with international and ethical evaluation best practice.
